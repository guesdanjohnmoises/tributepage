Manny Pacquiao: The People's Champ

About Manny Pacquiao

Manny “Pacman” Pacquiao — World Boxing Champion, Senator, and Filipino icon of perseverance and humility.

Emmanuel Dapidran Pacquiao, born December 17, 1978 in Kibawe, Bukidnon, rose from humble beginnings to become the only boxer in history to win world titles in eight different weight divisions. Beyond boxing, Pacquiao has served as a congressman and senator, dedicating his life to public service and helping the poor.

His story continues to inspire millions around the world — a true symbol of discipline, faith, and hard work.

Career Highlights

  • First and only eight-division world champion
  • 12 major world titles
  • Named "Fighter of the Decade" by WBO, WBC, and WBA
  • Ranked among the greatest boxers of all time

Legacy

Beyond boxing, Pacquiao is known for his public service, humanitarian work, and inspiring rise from poverty to becoming a global icon.
